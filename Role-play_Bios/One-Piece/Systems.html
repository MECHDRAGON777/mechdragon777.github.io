Technique Points (TP)


Technique points are awarded only via solo and group training as well as direct or indirect combat PvP. These points are typically rewarded by moderators upon reviewing your RP log or session, similarly for upgrading a pre-existing technique, you have to invent in (TP). I.e. upgrading a technique from D to C requires 2 (TP).

Below is a list of (TP) prices:

Attributes

Strength: Strength is a measure of a character's muscular or physical power as well as muscle coordinated work. This stat also helps keep hold of others, lift weight, grip, as well as force from ranged attacks (Not applicable to guns, but for slingshots, etc.). An appreciable level of this statistic is required for ranged fighters to use complex and powerful weapons. In addition, Strengthalso reduces the effort required for instance....a person is able to knock out his opponet with relatively higher strength. There can't be a huge difference between endurance and strength.

Agility: Agility is the measure of how quickly a character could move. It is also one of the major determinat factors for movement. Agility could be utilized for evasion techniques, but note that without enough perception, one would still be struck with an attack regardless of their agility. In addition, such characters are also capable of performing a shower of multiple attacks. As for ranged fighters, this is the ability to reload and fire at a faster rate.

Perception: Perception is the measure of a character and his/her perspective ability. It is a great attribute for ranged fighters as it primarily affects reaction time and aim. Although, a reasonable speed would also be required to evade attacks. It is an invaluable asset for ranged fighters with no doubt, but an appropriate amount of perception is also required for melee fighters, as one won't be able to land any ahits without this stat.

Intellect: Intellect is the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills as well as the faculty of reasoning and understanding objectively, especially with regard to abstract matters.

Endurance: This stat is the measure of one's durability and mental grit. Characters with a high value in this attribute are more likely to survive attacks, wounds, poisons or diseases. Endurance is the represenative of one's toughness and grit. Endurance is also a type of resistance against sickness and injury proneness.

Will: Willpower is ussualy control exerted to do something or resolution. In simpler terms it can be called Determination. Will is not a primary stat and had a differnt algorithm for it. One can gain 'Will' after Level 20 by engaging in quests at high stakes or heated encounters.

To The Grand Line

'To The Grand Line' is an exclusive RP mode, The core of the server that allows both marine squadrons and pirate crews to go on an adventure. There is much more to offer simply than just exploreing. You could find treasure chests, events, special NPCs, quests, and much more. One can team up with random characters to sail just once.

The Blues

The Blues are four oceans that cover much of the world: North Blue, East Blue, South Blue, and West Blue. They are divided by the Grand Line and Red Line and are dotted with islands that can be easily navigated using conventional navigation methods. In contrast to the hazardous Grand Line, the Blues are relatively peaceful. The islands in the Blues are rarely dangerous, the seas are calm and individuals of notable strength are rare.
  • Characters within the blues have unlimited freedom of travel. This means that a character may choose to visit any island within the Blue they started in.

Calm Belt

The Calm Belts are two areas that are just north and south of the Grand Line.

These areas almost never have ocean currents or winds blowing and are also nesting grounds for Sea Kings. They are very effective barriers for those trying to enter the Grand Line.

Weather

The currents and weather are extremely volatile, as they can change in an instant; this includes water spoutes, storms, blizzards, and other such things. Islands and the surrounding waters are the only areas where weather and currents are stabilized in a predictable manner. The reason for this is that islands on the Grand Line fall into four basic categories: Summer, Spring, Autumn, and Winter.

Deterioration

Is an important term. Deterioration is the process of becoming progressively worse. This factor is important to make roles such as a doctor or coook of some utility. There are many ways you could be deteriorated aslisted below.

Going without food in the Grand Line, you would deteriorate your health by 25% every few days, 50%, 75%, and face potential death gradually from starvation.

Paradise Entry

There are specific requirements to enter the Grand Line. Significant progression and story development must be made in the Blues before the entry. A crew is recomended to have all essential positions, a well-conditioned ship, a Navigator (II), and a crew average level of 13 (65 AP).

Reverse Mountain is an extremely tall mountain at one of the points where the four Blues meet and is one of the two known entrances to the Grand Line. It is located in the Red Line and is the geographical antipode of Mary Geoise and is also one of the obstacles that you must go through. However....an exception to this are the Marines who utilise Seastone-lined ships are able to conceal themselves from Sea Kings and have enough propulsion to cross the Calm Belt.

Routes

Navigation in the Grand Line does not follow the more conventional methods that would be used in the Blues. Due to the unique magnetic fields in the Grand Line, a Log Pose is required to travel to the next island in that Route. Tumbleweed is the first island whereas Flanders is the last. One must cross the Florian Triangle to approch the end of Paradise.

New World Entry

There are specific requirements to enter the New World. Significant progression and story development must be made in Paradise before the entry. A crew is recomended to have all essential positions, a well-conditioned ship, a Navigator (III), and a crew average level of 35 (175 AP).

The New World is entered via fishman-island and is a devastating place that could throw people back to square-one. The New World is also where the Yonko reside claiming Territories. The only way to survive is to either work with or against the yonko.

Professions

Some professions have specific advantages, Such as a shipwright who can be responsible for many duties and tasks on the ship or a navigator who is essential to travel to the Grand Line.
  • Captain: The title of Captain is the title given to an individual in control of a ship at sea and usually are the strongest members of their crew. Anyone can atain the rank of captaincy out of pure leadership, as a founding member, or voted by other crew members. All major decisions are made by them, though they may be usually advised and influenced by other members. Although a captain may be the highest authority of the crew, there are situations where they are betrayed, overthrown, and even killed in mutinies. Although rare, there are a few captains who take on a role within the crew in addition to their captaincy.
  • Combatants: These are the crewmates who are capable of fighting. This class is an exception to the professions system and are free of occupational (TP) costs.

    Tier 1: Combatant gets 3+ (TP) at start.
    Tier 2: A Combatant gets Double (TP) every five levels.
    Tier 3: A Combatant gets Double (TP) every odd level.

  • Navigator: A Navigator is the person on board a ship responsible for the navigation of the vessel. The navigator's responsbilities include planning the journey, advising the captain, and ensureing that hazards or obstacles (climat or otherwise) avoided. A Navigator must be skilled in using compasses, maps, and sea charts to their advantage both on and out of seas. They should be extremely knowledgeable about the world which is crucial when knowing where to sail and when due to weather patterns or otherwise that would impeed the trip.

    Voyager (I): The navigator is a rookie and knows the basic components of sea and the ship as well as weather.
    Cartographer (II): The navigator is an intermediate and is proficient cartographer capable of voyaging in Paradise.
    Sea-Tamer (III): The navigator is now a veteran and is experienced enough to guide the crew in the New World.
    (Note: If paired with a Helmsman, Travelling can be enhanced.)

They are responsible for the building and the repair of the ship; similar to how a ship's Doctor is responsible for the health of its crew, the Shipwright is responsible for the well being and seaworthiness of the ship itself. Often this position is shared with a Boatswain who would tend to the sails or other parts of the ship that do not directly deal with the foundation of a vessel. However, even the most advanced shipwrights cannot fix a boat that is beyond repair.

    Carpenter (II): The shipwright is capable of repairing a damage of 50% and adding innovative extensions.

    Welder (II): The Blacksmith can create weapons which are graded as (A-Class)
    Craftsman (III): The Blacksmith is experienced and capable of creating Skillful grade weapons.

  • Merchant: The right hand of the Quartermaster and the one who actually performs the task of spending, bargaining, and trading among the crew. Commonly a very sly individual, they are able to get better prices than most folks and know how to haggle. This individual can also be useful for scoping out a port town beforehand by sending them ahead to gather supplies rather than waiting to dock at port first.

    Dealer (I): A merchant purchasing items will give him 25% off as he's a regular trader.
    Wholesaler (II): A merchant will earn 2x the money for every job done.
    Retailer (III): A merchant will earn 3x the money for every job done, And will be able to trade exclusive items.

  • Scientist: The Scientist of a crew is a very rare position that is ever filled. When it is filled, however, it gives a crew access to someone with enough intellect to make the Blacksmith, Commando, and Shipwright dangerous individuals. Scientists can invent new armor, gadgets, modifications (Ship) or weapons for the crew to utilize that world has never seen before.

    Lab-Rat (I): The scientist is able to invent basic gadgets and Capable of giving a minor upgrade.
    Technician (II): The scientist is able to invent useful gadgets, Capable of enhancing intermediate upgrades.
    Engineer (III): The scientist is able to invent funky and advanced gadgets, Capable of enhancing add-ons greatly.

Crew Roles

Crew roles are passive roles to operate and maintain the ship, Such roles are passive meaning anyone can do it. They aren't of much significance, But can help you keep track of your personal adventure, inventory, and happenings in the sea.
  • Chronicler: A person who writes accounts of important or adventurous events.
  • Quatermaster: A person who supervises storage of food and items.
  • Treasurer: A person responsible for managing the finances of a crew.
  • Boatswain: A person responsible for the management of the crew and equipment of the ship.
  • Helmsman: A person who steers the ship or boat.
  • Scout: A person who is responsible for searching, making his crew aware of sightings and possible danger.

Workshop

This is a space for logging the work you do from your profession. This also includes you've crafted, used and the effects of the item regardless of how you intend to use it. This place will also allow you to craft special items.

Workshop can also be used to practice occupation I.e providing treatment to a stranger, helping a crew of sailors navigate, cooking on stands or restaurants for which you'll be awarded money depending on the amount of work you do. Below is the list of what each job can do:
  • Combatants: There is nothing significant this class can do, But they can always provide labour.
  • Navigator: One can create and sell maps, Navigate for freelance sailors.
  • Shipwright: One can repair vessels, Create and add modifications to a ship.
  • Cook: One can make mouth-watering dishes and also create banquets to feed people.
  • Doctor: One can provide medical treatment, First-aid and manufacture drugs.
  • Archaeologist: One can give history lessons, Read trivial books and go on treasure hunts.
  • Entertainer: One can perform acts, concerts, drama and more.
  • Beast Tamer: One can tame livestock for grazing and farming as well as tame beasts for prestigious hunt.
  • Blacksmit: One can forge and repair weapons.
  • Merchants: One can trade items from NPCs, Haggle prices, Sell items or lend items with clauses.

Scavenging

Scavenging is the method of searching, collecting items and materials as a resource. One can perform lumberjacking, fishing, herb farming, hunting, or treasure hunting; however, the items are random and always vary on differnt islands. REmember one cannot identify items as well as someone proficient with the profession, Intellegece plays a huge role in this. I.e. A shipwright being sent to find herbs.

Always remember that you cannot scavenge items in towns, one can always find a number of items in vegetative islands.
Beasts

Beats are creatures in the wild that could be hunted to yield meat or other rare obtainables with prestigious value. An archaeologist or beat tamer is required to identify rare beats.

A Beast Tamer can also be utilized to tame such creatures with unique abilities as well as trap animals to hunt them.

Beasts are classified in three types:


'C-Type' animals include those with little to no predatory threat such as reptiles, avians, rodents, fish, etc. They are of low value and often are the reason of plagues and annoyance to locals.

'B-Type' animals specifically include larger herbivores of great value as livestock. This classification includes various types of deer, bulls, farm animals, and large fish. They are of great value and could reward decently.

'A-Type' or the savage classification is one of the most vast and valuable kind. This also includes extremely predatory and savage creatures as well as rare and bothersome animals such as Humandrills, Blugori, Various Sea Kings, Flying Fish, Horned Shark, Big Tiger, etc.
