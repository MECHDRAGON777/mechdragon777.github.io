[ Backstory ]

Born on an island where winter is everyday, Kiko was the son of lumberjacks, appreciated in the village for bringing to the inhabitants logs to burn and warm up homes in this unforgiving climate.
Kiko himself, however, never truly understood why his parents worked their backs off for people who barely even paid them, or did anything in return at all. And so, instead of doing like his parents, he decided to learn to play the Biwa, an instrument that was fairly popular on the nearby islands…
However, wood and trees in general started to become rarer, until the villagers begged Kiko’s parents to go search for new ones further in the mountains. His parents, which were both absolute monsters in terms of resistance to cold, ended up complying and left in the constant snowstorm. They, however, never came back, and the villagers, thinking Kiko’s parents had left them to fend for themselves against the cold, took it out on him out of anger. Kiko, who thankfully had the same resistance to the cold as his parents, saw them coming, and fled, as the villagers opted to destroy his house to warm themselves up.
Some weeks later, words in the villages revealed that multiple cases of people disappearing after venturing out in the snow started to spread, as other rumors commented on the witnesses of a humanoid figure in the snow-storms, carrying a Biwa, and luring intruders with the music to kill them, either from frostbite in the storm, or from tiredness…
Needless to say that this figure, that the villagers suspected to be a ghost or an evil spirit, coming back to haunt them, was actually Kiko, who had started to take revenge on those who decided to send his parents away, and burn his house to the ground. Kiko himself started to enjoy this process, and soon became a bonafide psychopath, killing people for fun, as the thrill of the kill, anger towards others, and pain had morphed into a pure sense of sadism.
However, as he tried to kill one more person with his usual method, he noticed that this victim simply wasn’t scared of the cold, nor of him, they were just… ignoring everything. As his confusion grew, he revealed himself to the person, who laughed and told him “You want to kill ? Why stop at tired bystanders !? There are plenty of strong people out there in our world ! Some that defeating would change the state of the seas ! So, are you a coward, or a killer !?”
Touched by those words, Kiko decided to let his interlocutor live, as the latter continued to walk in the snow, coming out of the storm unscathed…
Kiko now travels on the sea, his trusty Biwa in hand, and he is determined to…
Have some fun ! What else !?

[ Fighting Style ]

Musical Spear: Biwa A Fighting style revolving around the use of a modified Biwa to be used as a spear.
History In the cold mountains, a certain psychopath, who used to lure poor trespassers during snowstorms by strumming the cords of a Biwa, decided to add a… sharper accent to his art, creating his fighting style out of pure sadism.
Musical Thrusts ~ Mahaha: This technique allows the user to send a flurry of thrusts at the enemy, creating a whole chord of music, the user can also end the technique with a stronger blow, resulting in a longer and louder note.
▰▱▱▱
Musical Thrusts ~ Chenoo: A technique that involves swinging the weapon wildly before unleashing a crushing overhead strike. The user's attacks are now harder to interrupt, and can break through an opponent's defenses quite easily.
▰▰▱▱
Sharp Art ~ Yuki Onna: The user is now fast enough to create two afterimages of themselves: One fights with the spear, the other plays the Biwa, distracting the opponent. The hits from the spear combined with the Biwa result in a whole concert, with each performance being unique. It is even more effective in misty areas or snowstorms, or even any environment that blocks the opponents view.
▰▰▰▱
Sharp Art ~ Kuraokami: The fighting style’s ultimate art. Each thrust of the spear and note produced by the strings sends a sound shockwave towards the opponent, wounding and weakening them further and blowing their ear-drums after too much exposure. The hits are also much faster and stronger than before, with the final hit of the final hit of **Musical Thrusts: Chenoo** being able to pierce through the opponent’s defenses
